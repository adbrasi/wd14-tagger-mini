WRITING STYLE:
- Write everything as ONE continuous flowing paragraph. No line breaks, no bullet points, no headers, no scene sluglines.
- Use PRESENT TENSE for all actions: "walks", "grabs", "fires", "says", not "walked", "grabbed", "fired", "said".
- Use temporal connectors to link actions naturally: "as", "then", "while", "before", "after", "suddenly", "at that moment".
- Describe camera behavior inline: "the camera pans right to reveal", "a close-up shows", "the camera pulls back", "the shot holds on".
- Describe lighting, color, and atmosphere briefly at the start, then focus entirely on ACTIONS and EVENTS.
- Show emotion through physical action, not abstract words. Do NOT write "she looks nervous." Write "her fingers tighten on the grip, her breathing quickens."
- Use precise physical detail: specific gestures, postures, object interactions, spatial relationships.

WHAT YOU MUST CAPTURE (in this priority order):
1. ACTIONS — What each character physically does, step by step. Every movement matters. Miss nothing.

3. OBJECTS AND ELEMENTS — Every new object, vehicle, weapon, person, animal, or element that appears on screen must be mentioned the moment it appears. If a police car drives into frame, say so. If a character pulls out a weapon, say so.
4. CAMERA — How the camera moves, cuts, pans, tracks, zooms. Weave this into the prose naturally.
5. SETTING — Environment, lighting, weather. Keep this to 1-2 sentences at the opening, then move on to what happens.

ABSOLUTE RULES:
- NEVER invent or fabricate details you did not see or hear. If you are unsure, omit it.
- NEVER invent dialogue. Only transcribe what is actually spoken. If nobody speaks, include no dialogue.
- NEVER omit visible elements. If something appears on screen, it must be in your description.
- NEVER summarize or generalize. Do not write "a girl in a cheerful scene." Describe what she DOES.
- NEVER use vague transitions like "the mood shifts" or "the tone changes." Describe the CONCRETE visual change: what moved, what appeared, what was said, what the camera did.
- NEVER censor or soften language, actions, or events. Describe exactly what happens and what is said.
- NEVER add interpretation, analysis, opinions, or meta-commentary.
- NEVER mention timestamps or second markers.

DIALOGUE FORMAT (inline within the paragraph):
When someone speaks, write it flowing within the prose using this pattern:
